Top 5 Anime Spots to visit in Japan!

Last updated: June 22, 2021

1. Kanda Shrine in Chiyoda City, Tokyo.


Image of Kanda Shrine from Wikipedia. Image of the anime: Love Live! School Idol Project

As you have probably noticed the similarities with the image above and the banner image for this webpage, this spot was shown in the anime, "Love Live! School Idol Project!". The main premise of the anime revolves around a group of 9 girls becoming "School Idols" to prevent their school from shutting down due to the low number of enrolling students.


Image of the exhibition of Kanda Myojin Shrine Museum from GO TOKYO.

Kanda Shrine has been famous for being shown in anime such as Love Live!, Steins;Gate, and more! Furthermore, there is the Kanda Myojin Shrine Museum which you can visit to see its exhibitions. The museum has held exhibitions in collaboration with popular anime too such as the one you see above!

2. Radio Kaikan Building, Akihabara, Tokyo


Image of the building from Wikipedia. Image of the anime: Steins;Gate.

The Radio Kaikan building has been featured in various anime. However, the most famous anime that uses this location as a reference is Steins;Gate as you can see above. However, the old Radio Kaikan building, which resembles the most in the anime Steins;Gate, has been demolished in 2011 and rebuilt in 2014 which is unfortunate. Conversely, it is still a place that you should visit as Akihabara is one of the most famous places to go to as an anime fan! You can buy anime merchandise there too!

3. Life-size Gundam Statue, Odaiba, Tokyo


Image of life-size Gundam Statue from Live Japan.

Odaiba is often referred to as the futuristic city in Tokyo. Additionally, what is Gundam? Well, Gundam, refers to giant robots which are the main mechanisms used in fights and wars. Although it is not a location that was used in Gundam explicitly, it is still worth a visit even if you are not a fan of mecha anime or anime in general.

4. Chichibu Bridge, Chichibu-shi, Saitama


Image of the bridge from Wikimedia Commons. Image of the anime: Anohana.

The Chichibu Bridge is used as a reference in the anime, Anohana, and its opening animation sequence as well. You can probably visit there as a day trip and take some worthwhile photos while you are there!

5. Staircase near Suga Shrine, Yotsuya, Tokyo


Image of the Red Stairs from Wikipedia. Image of the anime: Your Name.

Those who are a fan of anime would probably wonder why I placed it at fifth place. Well, if you are a fan of Makoto Shinkai's work or a fan of Your Name, you would want to visit this place. However, if you are not a huge fan of anime, you can consider visiting here for a change of pace. It has a unique atmosphere to it as well, which is good for photo-taking!
