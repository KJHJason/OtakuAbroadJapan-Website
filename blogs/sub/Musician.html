Japanese Musicians You Should Listen To!

Last updated: 22/6/2021

Introduction

Here lie some of my personal favourite Japanese musicians. Although the definition of Japanese "musician" may vary from person to person, personally it basically refers to anybody who originates from Japan and makes music for a living.

Here are some of my favourite Japanese musicians. Scroll down and take a look at some of the music that I like from various Japanese musicians. Note: Please reduce your computer volume when playing the embedded Spotify tracks as it can get quite loud.

Disclaimer for the images used: All images used here are not owned by OtakuAbroadJapan. Please refer to their respective credits.

Alternatively, you can open the embedded Spotify tracks on your Spotify app or Spotify website by clicking the Spotify logo on the top right of the embedded tracks.

Camellia


Portrait image from @cametek on Twitter.

Camellia whose real name is Masaya Oya (Japanese: 大箭将也; Oya Masaya) is a Japanese musician, born on September 28, 1992, is well known for his fast-paced music. He is very popular among rhythm gamers for having his music being hard to clear on rhythm games such as Osu!, Sound Voltex, and Robeats. His song mostly runs more than 200 BPM which makes his song difficult to clear on rhythm games. Fun fact: his song, "M1LLI0N PP" has an estimated of 24 genres and the BPM ranges from 125 to 234!

Below are some of my personal favourites. You should definitely give his music a try, especially if you like bass heavy music or fast paced music.

Spotify: Camellia

Yunomi


Portrait image from @CY8ERinfo on Twitter.

Yunomi (ゆのみ) is a DJ, lyricist, composer and arranger and has been active since 2015. He was born in Sapporo, Hokkaido, Japan. He would usually release music as a soloist or would invite other music artists to collaborate with him. He would also compose and write for other artists and groups too. He is also one of the more recent J-pop sensations to appear in the music industry, theming his songs around the concept of "cute". His music is heavily based on EDM, trap, and future bass or kawaii future bass.

Spotify: Yunomi

Egoist


Portrait image by Tomoyuki Yamasaki on ArtStation.

Egoist is a music duo, originally created for the anime, Guilty Crown. However, they have continued to release music separately since then. Egoist (band) consists of Chelly (Vocal) and Ryo (Producer) and has been active since 2011. The main genre that Egoist focuses on is J-pop and has also made music for various anime such as Pyscho-Pass. Below are some of my personal favourites songs from Egoist.

Spotify: Egoist

yanaginagi


Portrait image from her official website.

Nagi Yanagi (Japanese: やなぎなぎ; Yanagi Nagi) or yanaginagi is a Jpop singer-songwriter and composer signed under NBC Universal Entertainment Japan. She initially started by uploading cover songs on Nico Nico Douga under the name "Gazelle". She was then scouted by Ryo, the founder of supercell, and started working with him in 2009. However, she decided to quit supercell in 2011 and made a debut as a solo artist in 2012. Additionally, she mainly focuses on the Jpop genre for her music.

Spotify: yanaginagi

Kensuke Ushio


Portrait image from @agraph on Twitter.

Kensuke Ushio (Japanese: 牛尾憲輔; Ushio Kensuke) is a Japanese composer that goes by the stage name "agraph". He was born on March 1, 1983, and mainly does original soundtracks for various anime such as "Japan Sinks: 2020". He is also a rock and EBM (Electronic body music) and a member of the Japanese rock band, Lama.

Spotify: Kensuke Ushio

Nanahira


Portrait image from her YouTube channel.

Nanahira (ななひら) is well known for making covers with her loli voice. If you did not know, a loli basically refers to a female with a childlike appearance and would have a cute, high-pitched voice to sound like a little girl in the anime community.FBI, please don't arrest me. She is also capable of voice acting and would put in some dialogue to make the song more interesting to listen to such as songs like "Can I Friend You On Bassbook?". She would often collaborate with Camellia as her voice fits well with Camellia's music. Fun fact: She is also a virtual YouTuber (Vtuber) and streams occasionally.

Spotify: Nanahira

Hiroyuki Sawano


Portrait image from Moshi Moshi Nippon.

Hiroyuki Sawano (Japanese: 澤野弘之; Sawano Hiroyuki) is a Japanese musician and composer who is well known for making original soundtracks for various anime and movies. In 2014, he began a vocal song project named SawanoHiroyuki[nZk]. Additionally, he was born on September 12, 1980, and has been active since 2004.

Spotify: Hiroyuki Sawano

Honorable Mention: Shelter by Porter Robinson

Although Porter Robinson is not Japanese, I felt that his song, "Shelter" is worthy of being in this list. Please give it a listen if you have not listened to it before. It is honestly quite good!

Genres: Synth-pop, EDM.

Spotify: Porter Robinson
